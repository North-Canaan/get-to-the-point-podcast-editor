How the Hebrew podcast highlight editor works

The workflow is designed for long Hebrew interview podcasts where the final editorial judgment belongs to a person. Automation prepares the options; the human chooses the cut.

Ingest the episode

A URL can point to an RSS feed, an episode page, or direct audio. RSS feeds resolve to the most recent enclosure, while other URLs are resolved through yt-dlp.

Transcribe and diarize

The resolved audio URL is submitted to AssemblyAI for Hebrew transcription, diarization, and timestamped speaker-turn segments.

Find highlight candidates

Claude reads the diarized Hebrew transcript, infers host and guest roles, and proposes guest-centered highlights with Hebrew reasons and score values.

Review the candidates

The editor plays each segment, corrects roles, approves or rejects suggestions, and adjusts the start and end time of each approved highlight.

Produce the MP3

The review page loads ffmpeg.wasm in the browser, pads each approved segment, applies short fades, and exports a final ordered MP3 highlight edit.

Built for Hebrew-first audio editing workflows.
